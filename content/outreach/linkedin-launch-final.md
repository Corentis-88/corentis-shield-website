# LinkedIn launch final

## 1. Main launch post

AI adoption is moving quickly.

But in regulated workflows, the hard question is not only whether AI can produce useful output. It
is whether teams can control what happens before that output becomes a real-world action.

A draft reply is one thing. A customer message, case update, recommendation or workflow step is
another.

That is why I am building Corentis Shield: an AI checkpoint for regulated workflows.

The first wedge is financial services complaints and vulnerable-customer workflows. The goal is to
help teams test how AI-assisted work can be checked before action, routed to human review where it
matters, and evidenced as the workflow runs.

Corentis is early-stage, and I am looking for thoughtful design partner and investor/funder
conversations.

Happy to speak to anyone working on AI assurance, financial services operations, complaints,
vulnerable-customer support or regulated AI adoption.

## 3. More personal founder version

I keep coming back to one belief:

AI adoption in regulated environments will struggle if teams cannot control what AI does at the
point of action.

That is the reason behind Corentis Shield.

I am starting with a narrow problem: complaints and vulnerable-customer workflows in financial
services. The wider question is bigger: how do we make AI-assisted work controlled, reviewable and
evidenced before it reaches the real world?

Corentis is early-stage, and I am looking for design partner and investor/funder conversations
with people who care about practical AI assurance.

# Founder LinkedIn post options

## 1. Problem-led

AI is moving from answering questions to taking action.

In regulated workflows, that changes the risk.

A draft reply is one thing. A reply sent to a vulnerable customer, a record updated without review, or a complaint closed without enough evidence is another.

That is why I’m building Corentis Shield: an AI checkpoint for regulated workflows.

The goal is simple: check before action, route human review where it matters, and record evidence as the workflow runs.

Soft CTA: I’m speaking with design partners and AI assurance stakeholders.

## 2. Founder/building-in-public

I’m building Corentis around one belief:

AI adoption in regulated environments will fail if teams cannot control what AI does at the point of action.

The first wedge is deliberately narrow: complaints and vulnerable-customer workflows in financial services.

The broader problem is bigger: AI outputs need a checkpoint before they become real-world actions.

Soft CTA: If you are testing AI in a sensitive workflow, I’d be glad to compare notes.

## 3. Investor/funder angle

The next wave of AI infrastructure will not only be about better models.

It will also be about control: policies becoming checkpoints, human review being routed clearly, and evidence being captured as AI-assisted work happens.

Corentis Shield is an early-stage attempt to build that checkpoint layer for regulated workflows.

Soft CTA: I’m speaking with investors, funders and strategic partners interested in AI assurance infrastructure.

## 4. Design partner angle

You do not need to redesign every AI process to learn something useful.

Start with one sensitive workflow.

Test the AI outputs.
Find where human review is needed.
See what evidence should be captured before live use.

That is the design partner path for Corentis Shield.

Soft CTA: I’m looking for regulated teams open to a focused, non-production pilot conversation.

## 5. AI governance/action-boundary angle

A lot of AI governance focuses on policies, model choice and monitoring.

Those matter.

But there is another important point: the action boundary.

The moment where an AI output is about to become a customer message, case update, recommendation or workflow step.

Corentis Shield focuses on that boundary.
